About

The Tangram is one of China's most famous games. In the ninteenth century it found its way to Europe where it gained popularity in the coffee houses of the time. The classic Tangram has seven pieces (called Tans) arranged to form a square.

How to Play

The objective of the game is to rearrange the pieces to make diagrams of different objects (people, animals etc...), using all seven pieces and without any pieces overlapping each other.

History

While the classic seven-piece Tangram dates back to the early ninteenth century, it's origns may be over 900 years old. During the ancient Northern Song Dynasty, books of diagrams were published for table arrangements to seat guests at banquets, using seven tables of three different sizes.
